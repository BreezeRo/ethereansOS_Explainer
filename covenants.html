On-Chain General Purpose AMM Aggregator

To this end, we’ve already finished the first — and most crucial — phase of development. This was to create a set of general-purpose APIs that allow for any DeFi dApp to be integrated with Covenants-standardized AMM functions: Swap, Add Liquidity, and Remove Liquidity

Together, these APIs form part of an “AMM Aggregator” contract, which ensures that any AMM can be implemented with these functions (and other functions in the future).

Let’s take a closer look at how this works.

Swap; Add Liquidity; and Remove Liquidity. These are used in different ways by different AMMs. Uniswap’s router facilitates interaction with liquidity pools; Mooniswap forces you to connect with pools directly; Balancer allows for pools with more than two tokens; and so on.

Because of this diversity, the Aggregator must consider all of the AMMs together. That way, it can find common, cross-AMM rules of behavior, and thus standardize its operations.

The “Liquidity Provider Data” and “Liquidity To Swap Data” APIs enable this, by interacting with liquidity pool addresses to transmit their lists of tokens.

Additionally, every AMM interacts with ETH in a unique way. Uniswap uses WETH and ad-hoc calls, Mooniswap uses the zero address, Balancer doesn’t support it etc. We prefer Mooniswap’s approach, and have adopted it for the Liquidity Provider Data. For the Liquidity To Swap Data, there are specific flags when the swap involves ETH in the entrance or in the exit.

The Orchestrator is the extendible knowledge base that keeps track of all the supported AMMs. It works as an upgradable plugin system; there can be multiple plugins for one AMM, but every one will have a different version number and the associated upgraded features and bug fixes.

To begin with, the AMM Aggregator will deal with Uniswap v2, Mooniswap v1 and Balancer v1. However, anyone can code and propose an interface for any other AMM anytime, which if accepted can interact with any Covenants function.

General Purposes Liquidity Mining (Farming) Contracts

The new contracts will be highly flexible. Anyone will be able to use them: DFOs / ECOs, DAOs — even wallets. They will have programmable reward systems governable tiers of whitelisted tokens. And they’ll be far simpler to use than previous DFOhub staking contracts. These contracts will enable two different farming approaches:

Free Farming

Open-ended; block-based rewards shared among all farmers.

Locked Farming

Positions locked (:o) for specific periods; precise guaranteed rewards; limited availability.

Inflation Load Balancer

Any contract will be able to take both approaches at once. This will be managed by the Inflation Load Balancer, which does exactly what it sounds like.

Example: $HEWWO Farming Contract

Monthly Inflation: 10,000

Share allocated to free farming: 20% (2,000 $hewwo / month; 0.01 $hewwo / block)

Shares allocated to locked farming: [1] 30% (3,000 $hewwo / month; 0.015 $hewwo / block 3 months period) [2] 50% (5,000 $hewwo / month; 0.026 $hewwo/ block 1 year period)

For as long as the 3 Month and the 1 Year locked positions are completely empty, free farming rewards will be 10,000 $hewwo / month (0.052 $hewwo / block); as the locked positions begin to fill, the free farming rewards will decrease pro rata, until they hit the preset floor of 2,000 $hewwo / month (0.01 $hewwo / block).

General-Purpose Liquidity Inflation Manager

v1 Fixed Inflation has proven an innovative mechanism for startups to accumulate funds, allowing them to bypass problematic methods like ICOs and presales.

Using the upcoming general-purpose contracts, anyone (DAOs, DFOs, static smart contract extensions and wallets) can host governable fixed inflation systems with preset rules.

Powered by Covenants's AMM Aggregator, these contracts will have multi-AMM functionality and will be able to inflate both by Treasury or by Mint.

General Purposes Liquidity Crafting

Uniswap and most of the AMMs allows for liquidity pools composed of 50:50 asset ratios. This is usually the most secure setup for an AMM, but disincentivizes liquidity provision by incurring impermanent losses. Crafting resolves this, and opens up novel financial use cases for Uniswap pools. It is a fancy new way to build liquidity together, without needing to trust and know each other.Anyone can create a Craft order, customize the liquidity setup and deploy it by contributing some of the liquidity. Others can then contribute the rest required to pool the order on Uniswap. Later, after the predetermined block, any of the participants can trigger removal of the liquidity. It is then distributed to all order participants based on the predetermined rules.

UniFi Organization

UniFi is a Decentralized Flexible Organization (DFO) that researches and develops a responsible Decentralized Finance layer on top of Uniswap. Thanks to the DFOhub standard, Covenants dApps are entirely independent from any off-chain entity. The $UniFi voting token is a programmable equity of the Covenants DFO; $UniFi holders hold real equity of the protocol, and rule every part of its code and assets.

The Covenants DFO earn from The WUSD positive rebalance (the trading fees of Uniswap collateralized stablecoins). Portal will earn small fees (0.001% to 0.05% | 0% until the ECO update) by positions in Farming, Crafting, Fixed Inflation and a portion of the positive rebalance of the WUSD v2.
